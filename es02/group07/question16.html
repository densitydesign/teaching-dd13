Description

After our analysis with Crimson Hexagon we took in consideration a device previously ignored: Twitter. According to Crimson the majority of conversations about North Korean defectors were on Twitter, we decided to scrape all tweets from the last month and compare the number of retweets to see what were the most common contents. We observed a single peek in october where a great number of contents were shared. We noticed that all contents retweeted were about personal esperiences of defectors, even if other political contents had been shared on the platform.

Protocol

After a source breakdown analysis we downloaded all the most retweeted contents from last month, we created a trend line to highlight how social conversation was evolving, then we cleaned the dataset, organizing it in order to understand what were the most shared contents.
